The Commons

Personal health records will become the authoritative and supremely private representation of each of us in digital form. Healthy or not, most of us will want to safeguard and control what of our genome, family history, high resolution scans and medications can potentially reach all physicians and the most innovative of health care providers.

The Commons is a private and secure way for any patient to communicate specific portions of their personal health record with any health care provider that has access to the Internet, for free, using open and standardized digital documents that are readily accessible to both patients and physicians.

The Commons, as implemented by MedCommons, is also an inexpensive way of achieving interoperability among health information systems in hospitals, public health agencies and research labs without coercive credit-bureau-like information warehouses.

Using CXP, the Commons eXchange Protocol, the next generation of personal health records, enterprise health systems and medical devices will enable caregivers to respect patient privacy and honor their trust by offering import and export connections via The Commons.
