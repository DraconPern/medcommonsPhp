How It Works

MedCommons Inside is a collection of client and server components and services, available under license from MedCommons, Inc. A complete system is running and available for individual and unaffiliated users at www.medcommons.net, but most users work with a partner of MedCommons who has licensed theMedCommons Inside infrastructure and is running it in its own data centers, at a third party hosting facility, or at a range of hosts operated by MedCommons, Inc.

Every MedCommons licensee can proceed with confidence that any PHR account created for any patient or healthcare provider can be accessed from any MedCommons-enabled system. Each licensee is given a distinct pool of account numbers and PHRs to allocate as they see fit. Any user connecting to any MedCommons enabled site is automatically redirected to the correct PHR regardless of when and where it was created.

The Lightest Interfaces in the Industry


We've created the lightest components and interfaces with MedCommons Inside. Patients and Family Doctors access the system thru browsers. Radiologists and Cardiologists can connect to MedCommons from their existing DICOM workstations. We've hooked up personal healthcare appliances to flow directly into MedCommons accounts. For developers, MedCommons provides APIs and specifications for you to hook up your own devices. And, if you need to process credit cards we offer a flexible range of solutions.

Zero-Install AJAX Viewer
One page MedCommons Ajax Viewer interface for archival access and secure communications with any person or provider on or off MedCommons. Integrates CCR, PDF, DICOM, JPG, Secure Email Notification, Address List and Patient List, PIN access control.
Integrated Teleradiology
Accept Orders, Import DICOM, Create Reports, Export DICOM to and from diagnostic radiology and cardiology workstations and PACS. Preview reports in AJAX Viewer as they will appear to referring physicians. Integrated workflow status management for orders, reports and work in progress.
Device Interfaces
Integrated translation and import of measurements from blood pressure meters, glucometers and other personal health appliances.
Open Service Interfaces
Commons eXchange Protocol CXP 1.0. Developers access and update PHR elements using both SOAP and REST-style interfaces for tight integration with health care applications and services. MTOM support, for efficient transfer of images and other binary data objects. Registration services allow for the creating of both patient controlled and provider controlled accounts.
Credit Card Interface
One-time and recurring payments thru MedCommons or via your own merchant accounts.
